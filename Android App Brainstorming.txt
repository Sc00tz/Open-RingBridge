1. What to record (and how often)

Not everything needs the same resolution.

High-frequency (every 1–5 min if possible)
HR
HRV
Stress (since it’s derived from HRV usually)
Respiratory rate

👉 These benefit from trends and variability.

Medium frequency (every 10–30 min)
SpO2
Blood pressure (if it's actually estimated, treat carefully)
Blood glucose (almost certainly estimated → trend only, not medical)
Low frequency / event-based
Steps → accumulate continuously
Battery → only when it changes significantly
Sleep → session-based (store full breakdown per sleep session)
Workouts → store as sessions with high-resolution data inside
Storage tip (important)

Don’t store raw data forever at full resolution:

Keep raw for 24–72h
Downsample into:
5-min averages
hourly averages
daily summaries

This keeps your DB sane.

2. What you can calculate (this is where your app gets GOOD)

These are the real value features.

Recovery / readiness score

Combine:

HRV (higher = better)
Resting HR (lower = better)
Sleep score

👉 Output: “Readiness Score (0–100)”

Stress trends

You already have stress (0–100), but you can:

Calculate daily average stress
Detect spikes
Show “time spent in high stress”
Cardiovascular insights
Resting HR trend over time
HR recovery after workouts
HR zones during workouts
Sleep quality insights

Beyond just showing:

Sleep efficiency = sleep time / time in bed
Deep sleep %
REM %

👉 Then classify:

“Restorative sleep”
“Fragmented sleep”
Activity insights
Steps vs baseline
Active minutes
Calories (if reliable)
Respiratory health trends
Night respiration anomalies
Link with SpO2 dips
Correlations (this is POWERFUL)
Sleep vs HRV
Stress vs HRV
Steps vs sleep quality

👉 “You sleep 20% better on days you walk >7k steps”

Deep / Light / REM

📊 Trends Page

Let users pick:

Metric
Time range
⚙️ Device Page

4. Charts that actually matter

Avoid clutter. Use:

Line charts (most useful)
HR over day
HRV over week
Stress over day
Stacked bar (sleep)
Deep / Light / REM
Ring/progress charts
Steps goal
Sleep goal
Heatmaps (advanced but awesome)
Sleep quality over time
Stress patterns by hour/day
Correlation charts (optional advanced)
Scatter plots:
Sleep vs HRV
Steps vs sleep score
5. Goals & tracking

Make these flexible, not rigid.

Core goals
Steps (daily)
Sleep duration
Sleep score
Activity frequency (workouts/week)
Advanced goals
Maintain HRV above baseline
Reduce stress time
Improve sleep consistency
Streaks (very engaging)
“7 days hitting step goal”
“5 nights of good sleep”
Adaptive goals (VERY powerful)

Instead of fixed:

Increase step goal gradually
Adjust sleep target based on user behavior
6. Smart features (this is what makes it stand out)
Insights engine (simple rules-based is enough)

Examples:

“Your HRV dropped after poor sleep”
“You’re most stressed between 2–4 PM”
“You recover better on days with more steps”
Alerts
High stress sustained
Low SpO2 overnight
Elevated resting HR
Baselines (CRITICAL)

Compute per-user:

Normal HRV
Normal HR
Normal sleep

👉 Then compare everything to them, not generic values.

Tags / journaling (optional but huge)

Let users log:

Caffeine
Alcohol
Exercise
Mood

👉 Then correlate with data.

7. One important reality check

Some of these readings (especially):

Blood glucose
Blood pressure

…from a generic ring are likely estimates, not medical-grade.

👉 Position them as:

“Trends”
“Wellness indicators”

NOT medical advice.

UX DETAILS THAT WILL MATTER A LOT
1. Color logic (don’t skip this)
Green = good
Yellow = caution
Red = needs attention

Used for:

HRV vs baseline
Stress level
Sleep quality
2. Card-based design

Everything is:

Modular
Reorderable later
Easy to expand
3. Tap for detail

Each card → opens:

Full screen graph
Historical data
Insights
4. Keep insights SHORT

Bad:

“Your HRV is lower due to multiple factors…”

Good:

“HRV lower than usual. Likely due to poor sleep.”
